
**A single water molecule is an extraordinarily inefficient antenna at 489 nm.**

# QUESTION 2: Can We Model Signal Propagation Through Water's H-Bond Network?

In January 2016, a landmark paper by Elton & Fernández-Serra was published in [Nature Communications](https://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms10193):

> "Using molecular dynamics simulations, we find dispersive optical phonon-like modes exist in the librational and OH-stretching bands, and on subpicosecond timescales these modes **propagate through water's hydrogen-bond network over distances of up to 2 nm**."

Key findings:

| Property | Value | Significance |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Mode type | Optical phonon-like | Coherent, propagating |

**Water's H-bond network supports coherent, propagating modes — not just random thermal fluctuations.**

## 2.2 Energy Transfer via THz Excitation

A 2020 study in [Science Advances](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.aay7074) directly probed energy flow:

## 2.3 Charge Transfer Across the Network

> "Each water molecule participates in multiple H bonds, and each such bond influences other H bonds. Within each H bond, **electronic charge is shared.** The H bond network and the properties of water are also influenced by the **delocalization of protons**, a nuclear quantum effect."

This means:
- **Electrons are shared across H-bonds** — not confined to individual molecules
- **Protons tunnel quantum-mechanically** between water molecules
